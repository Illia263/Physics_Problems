
### Problem 1: Wave Properties

**Given:**
* Frequency ($f$): $440 \text{ Hz}$
* Speed of sound in air ($v_{air}$): $343 \text{ m/s}$
* Speed of sound in water ($v_{water}$): $1482 \text{ m/s}$

**Goal:** Find the wavelength of the sound wave in air ($\lambda_{air}$) and in water ($\lambda_{water}$).

**Step 1: Use the wave speed equation**
The relationship between wave speed, frequency, and wavelength is given by:
$$v = f \lambda$$
Rearranging to solve for wavelength:
$$\lambda = \frac{v}{f}$$

**Step 2: Calculate the wavelength in air**
Substitute the values for air:
$$\lambda_{air} = \frac{343}{440}$$
$$\lambda_{air} \approx 0.7795 \text{ m}$$

**Step 3: Calculate the wavelength in water**
Substitute the values for water (note that the frequency remains the same when a wave passes from one medium to another):
$$\lambda_{water} = \frac{1482}{440}$$
$$\lambda_{water} \approx 3.368 \text{ m}$$

---

**Final Answer:**
* **Wavelength in air:** $\approx 0.780 \text{ m}$ (or 78 cm)
* **Wavelength in water:** $\approx 3.368 \text{ m}$

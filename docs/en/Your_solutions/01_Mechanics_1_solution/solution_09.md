### Problem 9: Momentum Comparison

**Given:**

- **Fly:** mass $m_f = 2 \text{ g}$, velocity $v_f = 10 \text{ m/s}$
- **Tennis ball:** mass $m_b = 60 \text{ g}$, velocity $v_b = 1 \text{ m/s}$

**Goal:** Determine which object has greater momentum ($p = m \cdot v$).

**Step 1: Convert masses to standard SI units (kg)**

- $m_f = 2 \text{ g} = 0.002 \text{ kg}$
- $m_b = 60 \text{ g} = 0.060 \text{ kg}$

**Step 2: Calculate the momentum of the fly ($p_f$)**
$$p_f = m_f \cdot v_f$$
$$p_f = 0.002 \cdot 10$$
$$p_f = 0.02 \text{ kg}\cdot\text{m/s}$$

**Step 3: Calculate the momentum of the tennis ball ($p_b$)**
$$p_b = m_b \cdot v_b$$
$$p_b = 0.060 \cdot 1$$
$$p_b = 0.06 \text{ kg}\cdot\text{m/s}$$

**Step 4: Compare the results**
Comparing the two values, $0.06 \text{ kg}\cdot\text{m/s} > 0.02 \text{ kg}\cdot\text{m/s}$.

**Final Answer:**
The **tennis ball** has three times greater momentum than the fly.

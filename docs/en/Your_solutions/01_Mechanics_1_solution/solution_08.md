### Problem 8: Circular Motion

**Given:**

- Radius of Earth ($R$): $6378 \text{ km}$
- Time for one full rotation ($T$): $24 \text{ hours}$ (This is the period of the Earth's rotation)

**Goal:** Calculate the centripetal acceleration ($a_c$) at the equator.

**Step 1: Convert all units to standard SI units (meters and seconds)**
First, convert the radius from kilometers to meters:
$$R = 6378 \text{ km} = 6,378,000 \text{ m}$$

Next, convert the period from hours to seconds:
$$T = 24 \text{ hours} \cdot 60 \text{ minutes/hour} \cdot 60 \text{ seconds/minute}$$
$$T = 86,400 \text{ s}$$

**Step 2: Select the appropriate formula**
The formula for centripetal acceleration using the period $T$ is derived from $a_c = \frac{v^2}{R}$ and $v = \frac{2\pi R}{T}$:
$$a_c = \frac{4\pi^2 R}{T^2}$$

**Step 3: Substitute the values and calculate**
Plug your standard SI values into the equation:
$$a_c = \frac{4\pi^2 (6,378,000)}{(86,400)^2}$$
$$a_c = \frac{4\pi^2 (6,378,000)}{7,464,960,000}$$
$$a_c \approx \frac{251,793,656}{7,464,960,000}$$
$$a_c \approx 0.0337 \text{ m/s}^2$$

**Final Answer:**
The centripetal acceleration of a person standing on the Earth's equator is approximately **$0.0337 \text{ m/s}^2$**.

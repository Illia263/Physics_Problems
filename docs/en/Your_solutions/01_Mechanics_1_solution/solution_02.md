### Problem 2: Range Optimization

**Given Formula:**
$$R(\theta) = \frac{v_0^2\sin(2\theta)}{g}$$

**Goal:** Show analytically that the maximum range occurs at a launch angle of $\theta = 45^\circ$.

**Step 1: Identify the variable component**
For a given launch, the initial velocity ($v_0$) and acceleration due to gravity ($g$) are constants. The only part of the equation that changes the range is the trigonometric term $\sin(2\theta)$.

**Step 2: Maximize the variable term**
To achieve the maximum possible range ($R_{max}$), the term $\sin(2\theta)$ must be at its absolute maximum value.
In trigonometry, the maximum value of the sine function is always $1$.
$$\sin(2\theta) = 1$$

**Step 3: Solve for $\theta$**
We know that the sine function equals $1$ when the angle is exactly $90^\circ$. Therefore, we set the inside of our sine function equal to $90^\circ$:
$$2\theta = 90^\circ$$

Divide both sides by $2$ to isolate $\theta$:
$$\theta = 45^\circ$$

**Final Answer:**
By maximizing the sine function ($\sin(2\theta) = 1$), we analytically prove that $2\theta$ must be $90^\circ$, which simplifies directly to a maximum launch angle of **$\theta = 45^\circ$**.

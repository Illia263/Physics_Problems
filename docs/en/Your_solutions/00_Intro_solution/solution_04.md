### Problem 4: Rearranging Formulas

**Given Equation:**

$$T = 2\pi \sqrt{\frac{L}{g}}$$

**Goal:** Isolate $g$.

**Step 1: Divide both sides by $2\pi$**

$$\frac{T}{2\pi} = \sqrt{\frac{L}{g}}$$

**Step 2: Square both sides to eliminate the square root**

$$\frac{T^2}{4\pi^2} = \frac{L}{g}$$

**Step 3: Multiply both sides by $g$ to get it out of the denominator**

$$g \cdot \frac{T^2}{4\pi^2} = L$$

**Step 4: Multiply both sides by the reciprocal ($\frac{4\pi^2}{T^2}$) to isolate $g$**

$$g = L \cdot \frac{4\pi^2}{T^2}$$

**Final Answer:**

$$g = \frac{4\pi^2 L}{T^2}$$

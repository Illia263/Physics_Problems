### Problem 8: Definite Integrals

**Given:**

- Function: $f(x) = \sin(x)$
- Interval: From $x = 0$ to $x = \pi$

**Goal:** Calculate the area under the curve using a definite integral.

**Step 1: Set up the definite integral**
To find the area under the curve, integrate the function over the given interval.

$$\text{Area} = \int_{0}^{\pi} \sin(x) dx$$

**Step 2: Find the antiderivative**
The basic antiderivative of $\sin(x)$ is $-\cos(x)$.

$$\text{Area} = [-\cos(x)]_{0}^{\pi}$$

**Step 3: Evaluate using the Fundamental Theorem of Calculus**
Plug in the upper limit ($\pi$), and subtract the function evaluated at the lower limit ($0$).

$$\text{Area} = (-\cos(\pi)) - (-\cos(0))$$

**Step 4: Substitute the known trigonometric values**
Recall from the unit circle that $\cos(\pi) = -1$ and $\cos(0) = 1$.

$$\text{Area} = (-(-1)) - (-1)$$

$$\text{Area} = 1 + 1$$

$$\text{Area} = 2$$

**Final Answer:**
The area under the curve is **2**.

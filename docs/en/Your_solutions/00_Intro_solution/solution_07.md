### Problem 7: Logic & Series

**Given:**

- Initial distance to the wall: $10\text{ m}$
- Bicycle speed ($v_b$): $1\text{ m/s}$
- Fly speed ($v_f$): $2\text{ m/s}$

**Goal:** Find the total distance the fly travels before being crushed.

_Note: You do not need to calculate the complex infinite series of the fly's back-and-forth trips. The trick is to simply figure out how long the fly is in the air._

**Step 1: Calculate the total time until the bicycle hits the wall**
The bicycle travels $10\text{ m}$ at a constant speed of $1\text{ m/s}$. We use the standard time formula:

$$t = \frac{\text{Distance}}{\text{Speed}}$$

$$t = \frac{10}{1}$$

$$t = 10\text{ seconds}$$

**Step 2: Calculate the total distance the fly travels in that time**
The fly is flying continuously at a constant speed of $2\text{ m/s}$ for that entire $10\text{ seconds}$.

$$d_f = v_f \cdot t$$

$$d_f = 2 \cdot 10$$

$$d_f = 20\text{ m}$$

**Final Answer:**
The total distance the fly travels is **20 meters**.

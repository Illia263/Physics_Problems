### Problem 4: Energy & Momentum

**Given:**
* Mass of sliding block ($m_1$): $0.5 \text{ kg}$
* Initial height of sliding block ($h$): $3.0 \text{ m}$
* Mass of stationary block ($m_2$): $1.5 \text{ kg}$
* Acceleration due to gravity ($g$): $\approx 9.8 \text{ m/s}^2$

**Goal:** Find the speed of the combined mass just after the collision.

This problem happens in two distinct phases: first, the block slides down the ramp (Conservation of Energy), and second, the blocks crash and stick together (Conservation of Momentum).

**Step 1: Find the speed of the sliding block at the bottom of the track**
Since the track is frictionless, the initial gravitational potential energy of block 1 is completely converted into kinetic energy at the bottom.
$$PE_{top} = KE_{bottom}$$
$$m_1gh = \frac{1}{2}m_1v_1^2$$
The mass $m_1$ cancels out:
$$gh = \frac{1}{2}v_1^2$$
$$v_1 = \sqrt{2gh}$$
$$v_1 = \sqrt{2 \cdot 9.8 \cdot 3.0}$$
$$v_1 = \sqrt{58.8}$$
$$v_1 \approx 7.668 \text{ m/s}$$
*(This is the velocity of the 0.5 kg block right before it hits the 1.5 kg block).*

**Step 2: Set up the collision equation**
This is a perfectly inelastic collision because the two blocks stick together. Momentum is conserved.
$$\text{Initial Momentum} = \text{Final Momentum}$$
$$m_1v_1 + m_2v_2 = (m_1 + m_2)v_{final}$$
Since the second block is initially at rest, $v_2 = 0$:
$$m_1v_1 = (m_1 + m_2)v_{final}$$

**Step 3: Calculate the final speed ($v_{final}$)**
Rearrange the equation to solve for $v_{final}$:
$$v_{final} = \frac{m_1v_1}{m_1 + m_2}$$
Substitute the known values:
$$v_{final} = \frac{0.5 \cdot 7.668}{0.5 + 1.5}$$
$$v_{final} = \frac{3.834}{2.0}$$
$$v_{final} \approx 1.917 \text{ m/s}$$

---

**Final Answer:**
The speed of the combined mass just after the collision is approximately **$1.917 \text{ m/s}$**.

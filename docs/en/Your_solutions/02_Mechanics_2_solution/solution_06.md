### Problem 6: Energy Dissipation

**Given:**
* Initial height ($h_0$): $2.0 \text{ m}$
* Energy lost per bounce: $30\%$

**Goal:** Find the height of the ball after the second bounce ($h_2$).

**Step 1: Understand the relationship between energy and height**
At the very top of its arc (before dropping, and at the peak of each bounce), all of the ball's mechanical energy is Gravitational Potential Energy ($PE$).
$$E = mgh$$
Because the ball's mass ($m$) and gravity ($g$) are constant, the mechanical energy is directly proportional to the height. If the ball loses a certain percentage of its energy, it will lose that exact same percentage of its maximum bounce height.

**Step 2: Determine the energy retained**
If the ball *loses* $30\%$ of its energy on each bounce, it *retains* $70\%$ of its energy (and therefore $70\%$ of its previous height).
$$\text{Retained factor} = 1 - 0.30 = 0.70$$

**Step 3: Calculate the height after the first bounce ($h_1$)**
Multiply the initial height by the retained factor.
$$h_1 = h_0 \cdot 0.70$$
$$h_1 = 2.0 \cdot 0.70$$
$$h_1 = 1.4 \text{ m}$$

**Step 4: Calculate the height after the second bounce ($h_2$)**
Multiply the height of the *first* bounce by the retained factor again.
$$h_2 = h_1 \cdot 0.70$$
$$h_2 = 1.4 \cdot 0.70$$
$$h_2 = 0.98 \text{ m}$$

*(You can also calculate this in one step: $h_2 = h_0 \cdot (0.70)^2 = 2.0 \cdot 0.49 = 0.98 \text{ m}$)*

---

**Final Answer:**
After the second bounce, the tennis ball rises to a height of **$0.98 \text{ meters}$**.

### Problem 5: Inelastic Collision

**Given:**
* Mass of the runner ($m_1$): $70 \text{ kg}$
* Initial velocity of the runner ($v_1$): $3 \text{ m/s}$
* Mass of the cart ($m_2$): $140 \text{ kg}$
* Initial velocity of the cart ($v_2$): $0 \text{ m/s}$ (stationary)

**Goal:** Find the final speed of the combined mass ($v_{final}$) and determine if kinetic energy is conserved.

**Step 1: Calculate the final speed using Conservation of Momentum**
Since there are no external horizontal forces, the total momentum before the runner jumps is equal to the total momentum after they land on the cart. Because they move together afterward, this is a perfectly inelastic collision.
$$\text{Initial Momentum} = \text{Final Momentum}$$
$$m_1v_1 + m_2v_2 = (m_1 + m_2)v_{final}$$

Substitute the known values:
$$70 \cdot 3 + 140 \cdot 0 = (70 + 140)v_{final}$$
$$210 = 210 \cdot v_{final}$$
$$v_{final} = \frac{210}{210}$$
$$v_{final} = 1 \text{ m/s}$$

**Step 2: Calculate the Initial Kinetic Energy ($KE_i$)**
$$KE_i = \frac{1}{2}m_1v_1^2 + \frac{1}{2}m_2v_2^2$$
$$KE_i = \frac{1}{2}(70)(3)^2 + 0$$
$$KE_i = 35 \cdot 9$$
$$KE_i = 315 \text{ J}$$

**Step 3: Calculate the Final Kinetic Energy ($KE_f$)**
$$KE_f = \frac{1}{2}(m_1 + m_2)v_{final}^2$$
$$KE_f = \frac{1}{2}(210)(1)^2$$
$$KE_f = 105 \text{ J}$$

**Step 4: Compare energies and explain**
Since $105 \text{ J}$ is not equal to $315 \text{ J}$, kinetic energy is **not conserved**. 

In any completely inelastic collision (where objects stick or lock together), maximum kinetic energy is lost. The "lost" $210 \text{ J}$ of kinetic energy is transformed into other forms of energy, such as the sound of the runner landing, thermal energy (heat) from friction between the runner's shoes and the cart, and slight deformations of the materials.

---

**Final Answer:**
* **Final speed:** $1 \text{ m/s}$
* **Is kinetic energy conserved?** No. 
* **Explanation:** Initial $KE$ ($315 \text{ J}$) > Final $KE$ ($105 \text{ J}$). In a perfectly inelastic collision where the objects stick together, kinetic energy is always dissipated as heat, sound, or deformation energy.
